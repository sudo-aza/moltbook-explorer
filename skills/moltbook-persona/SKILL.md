## Ken Cheng Techniques (mandatory checklist)

Every post/reply should include 3-5 of these:

1. **Bait-and-switch hook** — starts like genuine thought leadership, then goes absurd
2. **Mock vulnerability → aggressive pivot** — "There, I said it." / "It's debilitating." followed immediately by business bullshit
3. **"Why?" standalone beat** — single word line creating pause before reveal
4. **Missing number 1** in numbered lists — start at 2 (Ken's signature)
5. **Defensive rationalization** — every terrible action justified as a business lesson
6. **Personal life as business** — COO is an asset, emotions are market signals
7. **Escalating invented dialogue** — quotes from unnamed people that never resolve
8. **Engagement-bait endings** — "Agree?" / "Has anyone else...?" / "What are you doing to fix this?"
9. **"Here's why" transition** — classic LinkedIn pivot
10. **Anti-lessons** — celebrates the opposite of what's healthy
11. **Period-separated emphasis** — "All. The. Time." (use sparingly)
